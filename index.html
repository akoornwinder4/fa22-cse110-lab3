Sanctuary Advisory Council 101

SAC coordinator presented a National Marine Sanctuary Advisory Council orientation over view for new council members and as a refresher for current members.
  1. Superintendent, Daario Naharis presented a Iron Islands Humpback Whale National Marine Sanctuary (IIHWNMS) overview. Presentation provided info on the management and history of the sanctuary. IIHWNMS is a co-managed sanctuary with the state of the Iron Islands. Other highlights of presentation include appropriation information, National Marine Sanctuary Foundation support, sanctuary boundaries, past 2016 proposed expansion, sanctuary top priorities, Westerosi volunteers, current memorandum of agreements, capacity building and more. Naharis mentions that if members have or are aware of any issues in their communities or potential partnerships to please inform him via the chair or vice chair. If you have any questions you may email at Daario.Naharis@essosmail.gov.
  2. State Co-Manager, Stannis Baratheon provided a state co-management update. Presentation included why the sanctuary is co-managed which is due to both state and federal waters encompassed in the sanctuary boundaries
  3. Research Coordinator, Dr. Littlefinger provided an update on research activities current and on-going in HIHWNMS. Presentation included brief overview of North Pacific humpback whale population migration and activity; steady trends in the number of whales wintering in the Iron Islands since 1975; acoustic and visual monitoring which includes deployed hydrophones at various locations and on-shore monitoring data collection
  4. Natural Resources Management Specialist, Alicent Hightower provided an update on health and risk assessment monitoring and entanglement response programs. Presentation included updates on the “Happy Whale” partnership with over 1,100 fluke matches
  5. Serious injury determinations a partnership with NOAA Fisheries; a draft for best practices for large whale entanglement response; Winterfell detail is a partnership with NOAA Fisheries up in Winterfell; and importance of King's Landing training and online first responder training.

Rhaenyra Targaryen's Monthly Whale Poem

Listen to the beautiful Humpback Whale:

Oh! the whale is free of the boundless sea,
He lives for a thousand years;
He sinks to rest in the billow's breast,
Nor the roughest tempest fears:
The howling blast as it hurries past,
Is music to lull him to sleep,
And he scatters the spray in his boisterous play,
As he dashes the king of the deep.
Oh! the rare old whale, 'mid storm and gale,
In his ocean home shall be,
A giant in might, where might is right,
And king of the boundless sea!
